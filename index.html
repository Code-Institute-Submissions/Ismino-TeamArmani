What is a social service dog?

A social service dog works together with its handler who together works for another person. Social service dog includes: Custodian dog, School dog, Therapy dog, Nursing dog, Teaching dog, Reading dog & Visiting dog.Therapy dog-works towards goal achievement. School dog works in schools and education. Care dog-jobs in care and social care.

Advantages of a social service dog

There are many advantages to social service dogs as they e.g. : Can counteract sitting at home and increase school attendance, support for the person to recognize their feelings, how and where they sit in the body, lower blood pressure, increase social skills, improve reading skills, improve wheelchair handling, etc. All this plus much much more, a social service dog can help people with.

A short History about social service dogs:

The dog has always been by man's side throughout the ages. It has existed and exists as a herding dog, it hunts and guards. Which the dog does today too. Already at the end of the 18th century, the first therapeutic activity with dogs is documented. The first study that exists where there is documented improvement in the work with dogs & sick children was published in 1962.
